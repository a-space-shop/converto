How to Edit a PDF Online

Upload your PDF by dragging it into the editor or clicking to browse. Once loaded, the full editing toolbar appears giving you immediate access to all annotation and editing tools. Add text anywhere on the page, highlight important sections, draw freehand annotations, insert shapes, and mark up your document exactly as needed. When you are done, click Download to save the edited PDF with all your changes applied. No software installation required.

The EditPDF online editor runs entirely within your web browser using modern browser PDF technology. This means your file is never sent to any server at any point during editing. Everything happens locally on your own device. This architecture makes it both faster and more private than cloud-based editors where your document travels back and forth to a remote server for every action. For sensitive documents — legal contracts, medical records, financial statements, confidential business materials — browser-side editing means your content never leaves your computer.

The most common use for an online PDF editor is annotating documents for review. Reviewers can add text comments directly onto the pages of a PDF report, highlight passages that need attention, draw arrows or circles to point out specific areas in diagrams or images, and add sticky-note style annotations without altering the underlying document structure. The result is a clearly marked-up version that can be shared with authors, colleagues, or clients who can immediately see exactly what needs to change.

Another major use case is filling in forms. Many PDF forms are distributed as static documents with field lines but no interactive form fields. With the text tool, you can click precisely on any field line or blank area and type your information directly onto the page. This works for government forms, application forms, questionnaires, and any other document that asks you to fill in information. When you download the completed PDF, all your entries are permanently embedded in the document just as if you had printed and handwritten them, but in crisp typed text.

The editor also handles signature workflows. You can draw your signature using the freehand drawing tool, or type your name in a signature-style font and position it precisely on a signature line. Combined with the date text tool, this lets you sign and date documents without printing, signing by hand, scanning, and emailing — saving significant time for routine agreements, approvals, and acknowledgements.

Frequently Asked Questions

What can I do with the PDF editor?
You can add typed text anywhere on the page, highlight content in colour, draw freehand lines and shapes, add rectangles, circles, and arrows, and annotate your PDF with comments. These tools cover the full range of typical document markup, form filling, and annotation needs without requiring Adobe Acrobat or any other software.
Do I need to install any software?
No. The editor runs entirely in your browser using modern web technologies. It works on any modern desktop or laptop browser including Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge. No plugins, no downloads, no installation. Open the page and start editing immediately.
Are my files kept private?
Yes, completely. All editing happens locally in your browser. Your PDF is never uploaded to any server at any point. This is fundamentally different from cloud-based editors that process your document on remote servers. Your file stays on your device throughout the entire editing session.
Can I save my edited PDF?
Yes. Click the Download button at any point to save your current work as a PDF. All text, highlights, drawings, and annotations are permanently embedded in the downloaded file. You can open it in any PDF viewer and all your edits will be visible.
What browsers are supported?
The editor is tested and works on Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge. We recommend the latest version of Chrome for the best performance and broadest feature support. The editor does not work on very old browsers that lack modern PDF rendering support.
Is there a file size limit?
There is no fixed file size limit since all processing happens in your browser rather than on a server. Very large PDFs with many high-resolution images may load more slowly depending on your device's RAM and processing power. Most standard business PDFs of up to 50MB load quickly on modern computers.
Can I edit the original text in a PDF?
The editor lets you add new text on top of the existing content but does not allow you to modify or delete the original underlying text in the PDF. This is a fundamental limitation of the PDF format — the original content is fixed. To edit the original text, use our PDF to Word converter to get a fully editable Word document, make your changes in Word, then convert back to PDF if needed.
Can I use the editor to sign documents?
Yes. Use the freehand drawing tool to draw your signature, or use the text tool to type your name on a signature line. Position it precisely where needed. The freehand tool responds to mouse and touchscreen input, so you can create a natural-looking signature. Download the signed PDF and it is ready to share or submit.
Is the PDF editor free?
Yes. Completely free with no limits, no account required, and no watermarks added to your documents. Open the editor, make your changes, and download the result. There are no restrictions on usage.
